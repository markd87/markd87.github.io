A short biography

I was born in Irkutsk, Russia. When I was 3 years old my family moved to Israel. In Israel I lived in Haifa for a few years, and then moved to Jerusalem where I spent most of my life, going to primary school and high school. At the age of 18 I was recruited to the Israeli army where I served in the navy for 3 years, both at sea on a combat ship and on land. Immediately after finishing my army service, I started studying Physics at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, where I had the great pleasure and honour of learning from great physicists such as Nir Shaviv, and Jacob Bekenstein to name a few, and the mathematician Ruth Lawrence. After the first year of studies I received the dean's prize and joined the university's honours program called 'Amirim'. During my studies I also worked at the computational quantum Chemistry centre at the University, doing small computational projects and being responsible of the centre and director's (Prof. Sason Shaik) websites. At the time, I was particularly interested in Astrophysics and Cosmology and I chose my research project for the bachelor's degree to be in the field of galaxy formation studies using cosmological simulations. After graduating I naturally continued my research in the same field with my advisor Prof. Avishai Dekel for the master's degree, titled "on the origin of angular momentum in galaxies". Following the master's degree, I decided to switch my interest to the field of condensed matter and Nanoscience. I wanted to experience life abroad so I started looking for PhD positions in various universities in Europe and USA, and was accepted to work with Prof. Vladimir Fal'ko at Lancaster University, UK. Following the first year we moved to the national graphene institute at the University of Manchester, where I am today. There I have the great privilege to work alongside the two Nobel laureates Konstantin Novoselov and Andrei Geim, as well as other visiting theoreticians and experimentalists.

• On 14/03/2018 I successfully defended my PhD thesis and passed the viva examination.

• Following the completion of the PhD I will be working as a strategic consultant - data science at Amey (named after its founder William Charles Amey), where data science is used to maintain, operate and improve the infrastructure around the UK and abroad. The infrastructure industry is normally considered as old and outdated despite its significant impact on the economic growth of any society, directly affecting the way people live, work and travel. As such utilizing the tools of data science and machine learning to the problems facing this industry can lead to significant improvements and innovation.
The job application process with Amey strategic consulting involved a phone interview followed by a take-home data analysis and report writing exercises followed by a whole day assessment centre attended by ~15 candidates and senior members of the Amey strategic consulting team. The day included another data analysis exercise, interview and a group activity.
