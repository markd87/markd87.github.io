A short biography

I was born in Irkutsk, Russia. When I was 3 years old my family moved to Israel. In Israel I lived in Haifa for a few years, and then moved to Jerusalem where I spent most of my life, going to primary school and high school. At the age of 18 I was recruited to the Israeli army where I served in the navy for 3 years, both at sea on a patrol and attack boat and on land. Immediately after finishing my army service, I started studying Physics at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, where I had the great pleasure of learning from great physicists such as Prof. Nir Shaviv, and Prof. Jacob Bekenstein to name a few, and the mathematical genius Prof. Ruth Lawrence. After the first year of studies I received the dean's prize and joined the university's honors program called 'Amirim'. During my studies I also worked at the computational quantum Chemistry center at the University, doing small computational projects and being responsible of the center and director's (Prof. Sason Shaik) websites. At the time, I was particularly interested in Astrophysics and Cosmology and I chose my research project for the bachelor's degree to be in the field of galaxy formation studies using cosmological simulations. After graduating I naturally continued my research in the same field with my advisor Prof. Avishai Dekel for the master's degree, titled "on the origin of angular momentum in galaxies". Following the master's degree, I decided to switch my interest to the field of condensed matter and Nanoscience. I wanted to experience life abroad so I started looking for PhD positions in various universities in Europe and USA, and was accepted to work with Prof. Vladimir Fal'ko at Lancaster University, UK. Following the first year we moved to the national graphene institute at the University of Manchester, where I am today. There I have the great privilege to work alongside the two Nobel laureates Konstantin Novoselov and Andrei Geim, as well as other visiting theoreticians and experimentalists.

Work and achievements

• During my time at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, working with the computational quantum Chemistry center, I contributed to a paper on no-pair bonding of high-spin Copper clusters, calculating bond lengths, and energies [paper].
• During my BSc studies, I've received the Dean's prize for the first year of studies, and appeared on the Dean's list for all three years. For my BSc degree project as part of the 'Amirim' program, I worked on the filamentary structure of the cosmic web in the early universe [paper].
• For my MSc thesis, I studied the angular momentum acquisition of disc galaxies through the filaments of the cosmic web and published a paper which was in the top 1% of the academic field of Space Science based on a highly cited threshold for the field and publication year.
• During my MSc studies I also worked as a postgraduate teaching assistant, primarily grading homework assignments for undergraduate Physics courses.

Other Interests

In addition to my scientific work and interests, I also enjoy expanding my knowledge in other subjects. Two particular interests of mine are web development and data science. Both are of great practical use and also fun considering the things one can achieve. As such over the years I've gained experience in: HTML, CSS, jQuery, PHP, and MySQL.
I discovered the field of data science and machine learning more recently, noticing the overall hype, and its wide range of applications in various fields. I decided it would be useful and beneficial to gain some practical knowledge in the field.
I learn and apply data science techniques with Python, and its various data science and machine learning libraries including scikit-learn and pandas.

I've successfully completed the Coursera machine learning course taught by Andrew Ng.
